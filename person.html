Salary record in plain language

    Method

    What each comparison is doing

    Purchasing power

    The first figure indexes real salary to the first observed year. Values below 100 mean the real value of pay has fallen from that starting point.

    Relative standing

    The percentile charts show where the record sits inside math, COSET, and the full SHSU salary file in each year.

    Statistical distance

    The z-score heatmap shows how far each year sits from the department, college, and university medians.

    Figure 1

    Purchasing power over time

    The first observed year is set to 100, so a drop below 100 shows lost purchasing power after inflation.

    How this figure was calculated

    The selected salary and comparison medians were first converted into FY2025 dollars. Each line is then indexed so its first available year equals 100; values below 100 mean the salary buys less than it did in the starting year.

    Figure 2

    Inflation-adjusted annual salary against internal medians

    This places the selected real annual salary beside the internal medians used throughout the memo.

    How this figure was calculated

    The selected annual base salary is converted into FY2025 dollars and plotted against the yearly median real base salary for the math department, COSET, and the full SHSU salary file.

    Figure 3

    Monthly pay compared with internal and external benchmarks

    This compares the selected estimated monthly base salary with SHSU internal medians and outside peer benchmarks.

    How this figure was calculated

    The selected monthly value is estimated as annual base salary divided by appointment months. Internal comparison lines use the same estimate for SHSU records; external peer lines use the official College Scorecard/IPEDS monthly faculty-salary benchmark.

    Figure 4

    Percentile standing inside each internal comparison group

    These percentiles show where the person sat inside the department, the college, and the broader SHSU file in each year.

    How this figure was calculated

    Percentile is calculated within each comparison group and fiscal year as the share of salaries less than or equal to the selected salary. For example, 70% means the record is at or above about 70% of that comparison group.

    Figure 5

    Department salary distribution with selected-person overlay

    This view shows the full department distribution and where the selected person landed inside it.

    How this figure was calculated

    Each annual box plot uses department base salaries converted into FY2025 dollars. The selected person is overlaid as a connected marker so the record can be read against the full departmental distribution for each year.

    Figure 6

    Base salary and additional compensation through time

    The stacked bars separate base salary from posted additional compensation where SHSU released that information.

    How this figure was calculated

    Base salary and posted extra compensation are converted into FY2025 dollars and stacked. Extra compensation is shown only where SHSU released a separate remuneration file for that fiscal year.

    Figure 7

    Standardized position across comparison groups

    The z-score view is a compact way to show how far a given record sits from each group's yearly center.

    How this figure was calculated

    The modified z-score compares the selected base salary to the median of each group for that year, scaled by median absolute deviation. Values near 0 are close to the group center; larger positive or negative values are farther from that center.
