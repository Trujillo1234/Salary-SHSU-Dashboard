Department Memo

Salary, purchasing power, and retention in SHSU Mathematics and Statistics

This memo organizes public salary and compensation records for SHSU Mathematics and Statistics. The central questions are whether pay has held its purchasing power, how compensation is distributed, and how the department compares with SHSU, Texas peers, and national public-university benchmarks.

Executive summary

The public SHSU salary files are treated here as a purchasing-power record, not only as a list of nominal salaries. The memo uses CPI-U to convert salary history into FY2025 dollars, then compares the math department with COSET, the full SHSU salary file, and public faculty-salary benchmarks for selected Texas and national peers. The latest salary file is FY26; the latest separate extra-compensation file is FY25.

Principal Findings

Findings supported by the current salary files

  1. Purchasing power. Inflation-adjusted median SHSU math base salary fell from $80,206 in FY2011 to $58,797 in FY2025, a real change of -26.7% in FY2025 dollars.
  2. Current roster. The latest SHSU math roster in the dataset is FY2026, with 58 employees and a median base salary of $50,949.
  3. Extra compensation. In FY2025, 5.4% of math-department employees received additional compensation beyond base salary.
  4. Texas comparison. Using the official 2024 College Scorecard/IPEDS monthly faculty-salary benchmark, SHSU's institution-wide figure was $8,196 per month versus a Texas peer median of $9,269.
  5. National comparison. Against the selected national public peers, SHSU's 2024 institution-wide monthly faculty salary was below the national peer median of $10,845.
  6. Large-record check. The highest total-compensation figure in the built dataset belongs to Holt, Melinda A. at $250,818.

Method

Three comparison layers

Purchasing power

Base salary and total compensation are converted into FY2025 dollars when the question is whether pay kept up with inflation.

Internal context

The department is compared with COSET and the full SHSU salary file so the math record is not read in isolation.

External benchmarks

Texas and national peer lines use the College Scorecard/IPEDS monthly faculty-salary measure, which is institution-wide rather than department-specific.

Scope

Claims supported by this memo

The memo is descriptive. It reports public salary files, public additional-compensation files, CPI-U inflation adjustment, and official institution-level faculty-salary benchmarks. The dataset covers posted salary amounts, real-dollar purchasing power, percentile standing, modified z-scores, and selected public benchmark comparisons.

FY2026 is included in the salary roster as a nominal salary year. Separate additional-compensation files and complete inflation-adjusted comparisons currently run through FY2025. Real-dollar trend claims therefore use FY2025 as the latest comparable endpoint.

Figure 1

Inflation-adjusted department salary bands

Salary bands are shown in FY2025 dollars so the figure tracks purchasing power rather than nominal pay.

How this figure was calculated

Each annual base salary was converted to FY2025 dollars using CPI-U. The dark line is the department median; the inner band is the 25th to 75th percentile range, and the outer band is the 10th to 90th percentile range.

Figure 2

Internal pay ladders across department, college, and university

These lines come from SHSU's posted salary files: math only, COSET, and the full SHSU file.

How this figure was calculated

For each fiscal year, the median annual base salary was calculated separately for the math department, COSET, and the full SHSU salary file, then converted into FY2025 dollars for purchasing-power comparison.

Figure 3

Total compensation and compensation reach

This figure combines base salary with posted extra compensation in the years where SHSU released a separate remuneration file.

How this figure was calculated

Total compensation equals posted base salary plus posted extra compensation. The left axis shows the department median total compensation in FY2025 dollars; the right axis shows the percentage of department records with any posted extra compensation.

Figure 4

Yearly salary distribution shape

The box plots show how the real salary distribution moved over time and make dispersion visible without a separate outlier list.

How this figure was calculated

Each box uses individual department base salaries converted into FY2025 dollars. The box spans the middle 50% of salaries, the center line is the median, and suspected outliers follow the standard 1.5-IQR box-plot rule.

Figure 5

Rank composition over time

Rank mix is included because changes in composition materially affect the department median.

How this figure was calculated

Each line segment is the share of the department roster in a rank group for that fiscal year. The shares are based on normalized title groupings such as Assistant Professor, Associate Professor, Professor, Lecturer, Visiting Faculty, and related groups.

Figure 6

External benchmark trend

The outside benchmark is the official College Scorecard/IPEDS monthly faculty salary for SHSU against selected Texas and national peers.

How this figure was calculated

The SHSU line is the official institution-level monthly faculty-salary benchmark. Texas and national lines are medians across selected public peer institutions. These benchmarks are institution-wide, not math-department-specific.
